KEY FINDINGS
  • All countries have spent more money on education and health care than the military.
  • The United States has the highest overall value for all expenditures.
  • China, India, South Korea are the fastest-growing countries in education and healthcare spending.
  • The United States has the highest levels of health and education per capita compared to GDP.
  • China increased most of its military spending from 2013 to 2018, which is $78 billion.
  • In most countries, per capita, health and education values are positively correlated with GDP.
